What Cloudera Learned About Macy's from Google
• The Google CE covering Macy's has been on the account ~6 months and currently covers both CE and FSR responsibilities due to a vacancy.
• The previous Google FSR/KE left the role the day after Christmas; replacement expected around April.
• This was the first interaction between Google and Cloudera on the Macy's account.
• Macy's already has petabytes of data stored in BigQuery.
• Macy's Google Cloud environment is considered relatively mature.
• Most new applications and workloads over the last seven years have been deployed directly on Google Cloud.
• Macy's on-prem environment still exists and is centered around a major Atlanta data center.
• The Atlanta data center contains several thousand virtual machines running legacy applications.
• Virtually none of that legacy data center infrastructure has migrated yet.
• Macy's has a formal long-term plan to migrate that environment to Google Cloud, beginning ~2027 and targeting completion ~2029.
• Macy's ultimate goal is to completely exit the data center by 2030.
• Many remaining on-prem applications are legacy systems not suitable for earlier cloud migrations.
• Google has limited visibility into the specific data workloads still running on-prem.
• Google primarily discusses analytics and AI initiatives with Macy's leadership today; conversations are typically centered on data already in BigQuery.
• Google maintains a regular cadence with the SVP of Data, Analytics, and AI at Macy's.
• The Cloudera workloads appear to sit under the infrastructure/database organization rather than the analytics team.
• The Big Data team that worked with Cloudera rolls into a VP responsible for infrastructure and data center strategy.
• That infrastructure VP is highly motivated to accelerate the move out of the Atlanta data center.
• If Cloudera can provide a low-disruption migration path from on-prem to GCP, that leadership would likely be interested.
• Macy's is extremely interested in AI and spent much of last year exploring use cases — including a multi-month internal hackathon where hundreds of employees built AI agents for finance, HR, supply chain, and operations.
• Current AI initiatives include contact center AI, AI chatbots for Macys.com, and enabling store managers to use AI insights for operational decisions.
• Google is positioning Gemini Enterprise, Vertex AI, and Model Garden to bring AI directly to Macy's data without moving it.
Next Step from the Call
• Google will help introduce Cloudera to the Macy's Big Data / infrastructure leader (Neil Chinta) to discuss the on-prem Cloudera workload and potential migration architecture to GCP.
